## Introduction

Pierogi are **traditional Polish dumplings** that can be filled with a variety of ingredients, such as potatoes, cheese, or meat. They are **boiled and then fried** for a crispy exterior, and are often served with **sour cream** or **fried onions**. Pierogi are a **comfort food** that’s perfect for any occasion.

## Step-by-Step Cooking Guide

### Step 1: Prepare the Dough

In a large bowl, mix **flour, eggs, water, and salt** to form a smooth dough. Knead the dough for **5-10 minutes**, then cover and let it rest for **30 minutes**.

<CookingStep action="mixing-dough-white" />

### Step 2: Prepare the Filling

Boil the **potatoes** until tender, then mash them. In a pan, sauté **chopped onion** in **butter** until golden. Mix the mashed potatoes with the sautéed onions and **grated cheddar cheese**. Season with **salt and pepper**.

<CookingStep action="mashed-potatoes" alt="Mashed potatoes" />

### Step 3: Assemble the Pierogi

Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut into circles. Place a spoonful of the potato filling in the center of each circle, fold the dough over, and seal the edges.

<CookingStep action="assemble-pierogi" alt="Assembling pierogi" />

### Step 4: Cook the Pierogi

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pierogi in batches until they float to the surface, then remove and drain.

<CookingStep action="boiling-polish-pierogi" />

### Step 5: Fry the Pierogi

Heat **butter** in a pan and fry the boiled pierogi until golden brown on both sides.

<CookingStep action="frying-pierogi" />

### Step 6: Serve & Enjoy!

Serve the pierogi hot with **sour cream** and **fried onions**.

## Final Thoughts

Pierogi are a **delicious and comforting dish** that’s perfect for any occasion. The combination of **soft dough and flavorful filling** makes this dish a favorite among Polish cuisine lovers. Enjoy this traditional dish with your family and friends!
