## Introduction

Bigos, also known as **Polish Hunter's Stew**, is a **hearty and flavorful dish** made with sauerkraut, fresh cabbage, and a variety of meats. This stew is **slow-cooked** to perfection, allowing the flavors to meld together. Bigos is a **traditional Polish dish** that’s perfect for cold winter days.

## Step-by-Step Cooking Guide

### Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Chop the **sauerkraut, fresh cabbage, pork shoulder, and kielbasa**. Soak the **dried mushrooms** in warm water for **30 minutes**, then chop them.

<CookingStep action="cutting_vegetables_lettuce" />

### Step 2: Cook the Meat

In a large pot, brown the **pork shoulder** and **kielbasa** in batches. Remove and set aside.

<CookingStep action="frying_pan_meat_red_cubes" />

### Step 3: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pot, sauté **chopped onions** until golden. Add the **chopped cabbage** and cook until softened.

<CookingStep action="frying_pan_vegetables_onions" />

### Step 4: Combine and Simmer

Add the **sauerkraut, tomato paste, dried mushrooms, bay leaf, allspice, salt, and pepper** to the pot. Return the browned meat to the pot and add enough water to cover the ingredients. Simmer for **1-2 hours**, stirring occasionally.

<CookingStep action="stewing_meat_red_with_veggies" />

### Step 5: Serve & Enjoy!

Serve the Bigos hot with **rye bread** or **mashed potatoes**.

## Final Thoughts

Bigos is a **hearty and flavorful stew** that’s perfect for cold winter days. The combination of **sauerkraut, fresh cabbage, and a variety of meats** creates a rich and satisfying dish. Enjoy this traditional Polish stew with your family and friends!
